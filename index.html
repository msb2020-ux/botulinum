🎨 Aesthetic Applications

Upper Third of Face

Glabellar Complex: 5-point injection pattern targeting procerus and corrugator muscles. Typical dosing: 20-25 units Botox, 50-60 units Dysport.

Forehead Lines: Horizontal injection pattern across frontalis muscle, avoiding brow ptosis. Dosing: 10-20 units Botox, 25-50 units Dysport.

Crow's Feet: 3-point lateral canthal injection, targeting orbicularis oculi. Bilateral dosing: 12-24 units Botox, 30-60 units Dysport.

Pro Tip: Always assess muscle thickness and activity level before determining final dosing.

Mid-Face Rejuvenation

Bunny Lines: Nasalis muscle targeting for nasal scrunch reduction. Conservative dosing: 2-4 units Botox per side.

Gummy Smile: Levator labii superioris injection to reduce excessive gingival display. Precise placement crucial: 1-2 units Botox per side.

Nose Tip Lift: Depressor septi nasi targeting for subtle nasal tip elevation. Advanced technique: 2-4 units Botox.

Pro Tip: Mid-face treatments require conservative dosing to maintain natural expression.

Lower Face Contouring

Masseter Reduction: Facial slimming through masseter muscle relaxation. Higher dosing: 25-50 units Botox per side.

Mentalis Treatment: Chin dimpling reduction via mentalis muscle relaxation. Conservative approach: 2-6 units Botox.

DAO Targeting: Depressor anguli oris for corner mouth lift. Precise injection: 2-5 units Botox per side.

Pro Tip: Lower face treatments can significantly impact smile dynamics — always demonstrate expected changes.

Advanced Neck & Décolletage

Platysmal Bands: Vertical neck band reduction through platysma targeting. Higher volume: 25-100 units Botox total.

Nefertiti Lift: Lower face and jawline contouring via platysma relaxation. Advanced technique: 20-50 units Botox.

Décolletage Lines: Chest wrinkle reduction through superficial injection. Emerging application: 25-50 units Botox.

Risk Management & Safety Protocols

Protect your practice and patients with comprehensive safety guidelines

🚫 Absolute Contraindications

  • Known hypersensitivity to botulinum toxin
  • Infection at proposed injection sites
  • Myasthenia gravis or Lambert-Eaton syndrome
  • Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS)
  • Active pregnancy or breastfeeding (aesthetic use)
  • Concurrent aminoglycoside antibiotics
Legal Protection: Proper contraindication screening is essential for malpractice defense.

⚠️ Relative Contraindications

  • Neuromuscular junction disorders
  • Chronic respiratory compromise
  • Concurrent anticholinergic medications
  • Previous adverse reactions to botulinum toxin
  • Coagulation disorders or anticoagulant therapy
  • Body dysmorphic disorder (aesthetic applications)
Risk Assessment: Detailed documentation required for relative contraindications.

📊 Adverse Effects by Frequency

Common (>10%)

  • Injection site pain, bruising, swelling
  • Headache (typically resolves 24-48 hours)
  • Mild asymmetry or overcorrection

Less Common (1-10%)

  • Brow or eyelid ptosis
  • Dry mouth or difficulty swallowing
  • Diplopia or blurred vision
  • Flu-like symptoms

Rare (<1%)

  • Systemic muscle weakness
  • Respiratory compromise
  • Anaphylactic reactions

🚨 Emergency Management

  • Immediate airway assessment if respiratory symptoms
  • Epinephrine administration for anaphylaxis
  • Supportive care and hospitalization if systemic effects
  • Antitoxin consideration for severe systemic toxicity
